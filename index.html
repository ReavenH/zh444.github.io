Brief Bio

"Automation is no longer just a problem for those working in manufacturing. Physical labor was replaced by robots; mental labor is going to be replacedby Al and software."

I am currently a Master of Engineering student at the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering of Cornell University. My current study focuses on the perception/estimation techniques applied in quadruped robots including the visual odometry and SLAM. I am a member of Napp Lab and the CEI Lab, supervised by Prof. Nils Napp and Prof. Kirstin Petersen. The goal of my research group is to build a quadruped robot (maybe a swarm) which is able to implement automatic construction tasks. Prior to joining Cornell, I received my Bachelor of Engineering Degree in Telecommunications from Tiangong University, China.
